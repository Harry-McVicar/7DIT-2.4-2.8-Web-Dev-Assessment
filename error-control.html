Error Control

Error Control is the process by which errors are detected and corrected in data ranging from bar codes to bank details and is hence essential to all computational systems. Throughout this concept you will learn about barcodes and checksums, human error, error controlling algorithms and parity bits.

What is error control and why is it so important?

Errors occur in digital data as a result of data corruption, human error, data decay and transmintion data corruption. Data corruption involves errors in computer data that occur during writing, reading, storage, transmition or processing that result in unintentional changes. Human errors often occur during data entry with individuals typing in incorrect information. Data decay may occur to stored data as a result of failing circuits or from bits losing their magnetic orientation or electrical charge. Transmition data corruption occurs when transmition is interupted by either interfering waves or poor environmental conditions and results in incorrect data being recieved.
